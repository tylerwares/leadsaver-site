Privacy Policy

Effective date: October 4, 2025 · Last updated: October 4, 2025

Leadsaver (“we”, “us”, “our”) provides an automated missed-call text response service for small businesses (the “Service”). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

1) Information We Collect

  • Business account data: name, email, phone number(s), company name, billing details.
  • Customer contact data: phone numbers that call your business, SMS content you configure and send via the Service, timestamps, delivery/reporting metadata.
  • Technical data: device, browser, IP, log files, diagnostic information, cookies or similar tech on our website.
  • Payment data: processed by our payment provider; we do not store full card numbers.

2) How We Use Information

  • Provide and operate the Service (e.g., detect missed calls and send your auto-reply SMS).
  • Monitor deliverability and performance; troubleshoot and improve features.
  • Billing, account management, and customer support.
  • Security, fraud prevention, and compliance with legal obligations.
  • With your consent, to send product updates or marketing (you may opt out at any time).

3) Legal Bases (if applicable)

Where required (e.g., EU/UK), we rely on: performance of a contract, legitimate interests (running and improving the Service), consent (marketing), and legal obligations.

4) SMS & Calling Data

When enabled, Leadsaver interacts with telephony providers (e.g., Twilio) to detect missed calls and send your pre-set SMS. We process:

  • Caller phone numbers and timestamps.
  • Message content you configure and any replies from your customers.
  • Delivery status and error codes.

Your responsibility: You must ensure you have the right to message your customers (e.g., consent where required under TCPA/CTIA in the U.S., CASL in Canada) and that your templates comply with applicable laws and carrier policies.
